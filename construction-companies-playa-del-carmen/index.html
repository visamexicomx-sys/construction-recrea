How to Choose a Construction Company in Playa del Carmen

Playa del Carmen grew faster than almost any city in Mexico, and the boom brought dozens of improvised "builders." Choosing wrong costs months of delay, cost overruns, and work that won't pass a structural review. Before you sign with any of the construction companies in Playa del Carmen, verify these 5 non-negotiables:

Construction license and DRO

Every legal build needs a municipal license and a Responsible Works Director (DRO) with a license number. Without a DRO the work is illegal and the addition cannot be registered on the deed.

Workers registered with IMSS

If a worker is injured and uninsured, the legal and financial liability falls on you, the landowner. Require IMSS employer registration.

Fixed-price contract

Detailed scope, milestone payment schedule and a delay penalty. Avoid open-ended "cost-plus" budgets with no cap — they're a blank check.

Verifiable portfolio

Ask for real addresses of finished projects and talk to past clients. Generic stock photos don't count.

Written warranty

A serious company backs its work with a written structural warranty. Recrea provides a 1-year written warranty on every project.

Everything under one roof

Architecture, permits, construction, electrical, carpentry and ironwork in one company stops subcontractors from blaming each other.
