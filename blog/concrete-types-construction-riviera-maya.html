Tropical Climate Recommendations

  • Minimum f'c 250 for any structural element
  • Minimum 5 cm steel cover in coastal zone (vs 2.5 cm standard)
  • Minimum 7-day wet curing due to accelerated evaporation
  • Integral waterproofing additives for cisterns and pools

Frequently Asked Questions

Ready-mix (premezclado): f'c 200–300 kg/cm², delivered by truck, most common for residential. Shotcrete/gunite: sprayed application for pools and retaining walls. Lightweight concrete: for second floors to reduce structural load. Self-compacting (SCC): for complex forms with dense rebar.

Foundation: f'c 250 kg/cm² minimum. Columns and beams: f'c 250–300 for hurricane resistance. Slabs: f'c 200–250. Pool shells: f'c 250 with waterproofing admixture. Quintana Roo code mandates f'c 200 minimum for structural elements.

Ready-mix delivered: $2,800–$3,500 MXN/m³ in Playa del Carmen. $3,000–$4,000 in Tulum (transport premium). Pumping service: $1,500–$3,000 extra for upper floors. Fiber-reinforced: +$300–$500/m³. A typical 150m² home uses 80–120 m³ of concrete.

Rapid evaporation in 35°C heat causes shrinkage cracking. Solutions: wet curing for 7+ days (we cover with burlap and water), pour in early morning, use curing compound, control joints at 3m intervals, fiber mesh reinforcement for slabs.

Salt air corrodes standard rebar in 5–10 years if concrete cover is insufficient. We use: minimum 3cm cover (vs 2.5cm code minimum), anti-corrosion rebar coating for beachfront, high-density concrete to reduce chloride penetration. This is the #1 long-term quality issue on the coast.

Our 6-Step Process

01
Structural Engineering

Calculate load requirements, select concrete strengths, design rebar layout per NOM-031 plus our enhanced coastal standards.

02
Material Sourcing

Coordinate with local batching plants (CEMEX, Holcim). Schedule deliveries to avoid traffic delays and heat.

03
Formwork

Build forms for foundation, columns, beams, slabs. Verify dimensions and rebar placement before pour.

04
Pour & Vibration

Concrete placed, vibrated to eliminate air pockets, leveled. Critical: pour before mix sets (90 min in heat).

05
Curing

7-day minimum wet curing. Burlap covering, regular watering. No shortcuts — curing determines final strength.

06
Testing & Quality

Cylinder samples tested at 7 and 28 days in certified lab. We verify every pour meets specified strength.
