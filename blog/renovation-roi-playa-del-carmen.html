5 High-Impact, Low-Cost Upgrades

Under $3,000 each — big visual impact:

  1. Chukum plaster walls ($1,500–$2,500) — the signature Riviera Maya finish, photographs beautifully for Airbnb
  2. LED landscape lighting ($800–$1,500) — transforms the property at night, especially pool areas
  3. Tropical garden ($1,000–$2,000) — palms, bougainvillea, bird of paradise — instant "jungle chic"
  4. Outdoor rain shower ($500–$1,200) — Instagram-worthy feature that guests love
  5. Custom wooden doors/accents ($1,000–$3,000) — tzalam or parota wood elevates the entire feel

Renovation Mistakes That Destroy Value

  1. Cheap waterproofing — in Riviera Maya humidity, bad waterproofing means mold within 2 years
  2. Closing off outdoor spaces — buyers and renters pay premium for open-air living. Don't wall it in.
  3. Over-personalizing — loud tile patterns and custom murals hurt resale. Keep it neutral + tropical.
  4. Ignoring drainage — heavy rains will flood your new renovation if drainage isn't engineered properly
  5. Using US/European materials — imported materials cost 2–3× more. Local equivalents perform better in this climate.
