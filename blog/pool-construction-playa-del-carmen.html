Building a Pool in Playa del Carmen: Costs, Permits, and Timeline

Updated June 2026 • By Recrea Construction • 8 min read

Quick answer: Pool construction in Playa del Carmen costs $8,000–$35,000+ USD. Plunge pool: 3–4 weeks. Standard pool: 5–7 weeks. Infinity edge: 6–10 weeks.

Riviera Maya Pool Considerations

High Water Table

Playa del Carmen sits on porous limestone with a high water table, often just 1-3 meters below the surface. This affects pool construction in several ways:

  • Excavation hits water quickly — dewatering pumps are needed during construction
  • The pool shell must withstand hydrostatic pressure from below (groundwater pushing up)
  • Drainage systems must account for the water table level during rainy season
  • Some areas require thicker pool shells and additional waterproofing

Hurricane Season

Hurricane season (June–November) brings heavy rain. Your pool needs an overflow drainage system that handles sudden water volume increases. Without it, pool water mixes with rain runoff and floods surrounding areas.

Permits Required

  • Municipal construction license — required for any structural work including pools. Applied for at the Direccion de Obras Publicas in Solidaridad municipality.
  • Environmental assessment — may be required depending on your property's proximity to protected areas, cenotes, or the coastline.
  • HOA approval — if your property is in a gated community or development, you likely need homeowner association approval before construction begins.

Pool Materials

  • Reinforced concrete (most common) — poured concrete with steel rebar cage. The standard in Mexico. Durable, fully customizable shape, works with any finish.
  • Pool finishes: Blue mosaic tile (classic), chukum plaster (natural look), pebble aggregate (durable), quartz plaster (smooth).
  • Equipment: Variable-speed pump (saves 50-70% on electricity), salt chlorine generator (gentler than chemical chlorine), LED lighting, solar heater (extends swimming season).

Monthly Maintenance Costs

  • Chemicals and water treatment: $50–$100 USD/month
  • Electricity (pump): $30–$80 USD/month (variable-speed pump saves significantly)
  • Professional cleaning service: $80–$150 USD/month (2-3 visits per week)
  • Total: approximately $160–$330 USD/month

5 Pool Construction Mistakes

  1. No soil/geology study — hitting a cenote (underground cave) during excavation is a real possibility in PDC. A geological study ($500-$1,000) prevents a $10,000+ surprise.
  2. Undersized overflow system — during tropical storms, a pool without proper overflow floods your property and possibly your neighbors'.
  3. Single-speed pump — wastes electricity. A variable-speed pump costs more upfront but saves 50-70% on electricity bills.
  4. Skipping waterproofing on the pool deck — water seeping under the pool deck causes erosion and deck cracking.
  5. No shade planning — full-sun pools in Playa del Carmen are uncomable from 11am-3pm. Plan for a palapa, pergola, or shade sails from the start.
