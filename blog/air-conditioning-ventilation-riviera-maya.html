Air Conditioning & Ventilation in the Riviera Maya 2026

With year-round temperatures of 28-35°C and 70-90% humidity, AC is essential in the Riviera Maya. A well-planned installation during construction saves up to 40% on energy costs vs. a retrofit.

Inverter vs Conventional

  • Energy savings: 30-50% vs conventional
  • Lifespan: 15-20 years vs 8-12
  • Extra cost: 40-60% more upfront
  • ROI: pays for itself in 2-3 years with daily use

BTU Sizing (Tropical Rule)

  • 800-1,000 BTU per m² (vs 600 in temperate climates)
  • High ceilings (+3m): add 20%
  • Large windows: add 15-25%
  • Kitchen: add 4,000 BTU
  • Top floor: add 20% for roof solar radiation

Frequently Asked Questions

Mini-split inverter units (Mirage, Carrier, Daikin) are the standard. 12,000 BTU per 15–20m². Inverter technology uses 30–40% less electricity than conventional. Multi-zone systems for 3+ bedrooms. Central ducted A/C only for luxury villas over 200m².

Single mini-split 12,000 BTU: $12,000–$18,000 MXN installed. 18,000 BTU: $16,000–$24,000. Multi-zone (3 heads): $45,000–$80,000. Central ducted system: $120,000–$300,000. VRF commercial: $200,000–$600,000.

Cross-ventilation design can reduce A/C use by 40–60%. Orient openings to prevailing east winds. High ceilings (3m+) let hot air rise. Ceiling fans: $3,000–$8,000 each. Louver windows: best for airflow. Works great Oct–Apr, A/C still needed May–Sep.

Running one 12,000 BTU unit 8 hrs/day: $1,200–$2,000 MXN/month. Full home (3 units): $3,000–$6,000/month. Inverter units save 30–40%. Solar panels can offset 50–80% of A/C costs. CFE DAC tariff penalty doubles cost if consumption exceeds threshold.

Filter cleaning: monthly (salt air clogs fast). Full service: every 6 months — refrigerant check, coil cleaning, drain line flush. Coastal units corrode faster; use anti-corrosion coating on outdoor unit. Budget $1,500–$3,000 per service per unit.

Our 6-Step Process

01
Climate Assessment

Analyze your property's solar exposure, prevailing winds, and room layout to size A/C correctly.

02
System Design

Select equipment, plan ductwork or line routing, calculate BTU per room. Oversizing wastes energy; undersizing fails in summer.

03
Electrical Preparation

Verify panel capacity. Each mini-split needs a dedicated 220V circuit. Upgrade panel if needed ($15,000–$30,000 MXN).

04
Installation

Mount indoor/outdoor units, run copper lines, install drainage. Professional installation prevents refrigerant leaks.

05
Commissioning

Test all units, verify cooling capacity, set thermostats, check drainage. Demonstrate operation to owner.

06
Maintenance Plan

Semi-annual service contract. Filter reminders. Anti-corrosion treatment for coastal properties.
