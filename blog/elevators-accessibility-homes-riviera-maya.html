Universal Accessibility

  • Ramps: max 8% slope, min 1.20 m width, $150–$400 USD/linear meter
  • Doors: min 90 cm width, lever handles
  • Bathrooms: 1.50 m turning radius, grab bars, curbless shower
  • Floors: non-slip, no abrupt level changes

Regulations

  • NOM-001-SSA3: building accessibility standards
  • Municipal code: commercial and multi-family 3+ stories require elevator
  • Maintenance: mandatory inspection every 6 months

NOM-233 requires accessibility in public buildings: ramps (max 8% slope), 90cm minimum door width, accessible bathroom (150cm turning radius), tactile floor indicators. Private homes: no legal requirements, but we recommend universal design for resale value and aging-in-place.

Designing homes usable by everyone regardless of age or ability: 90cm+ doorways, zero-threshold showers, lever handles, single-floor living option. Costs 2–5% more during construction but adds significant resale value and prevents expensive retrofits later.
