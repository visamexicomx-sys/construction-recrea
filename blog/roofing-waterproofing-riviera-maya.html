Frequently Asked Questions

Reinforced concrete slab (losa): the standard for hurricane zones. No tile, no shingle — concrete only for permanent structures. Slope: 2–3% minimum for drainage. Palapa (palm thatch): for covered outdoor areas only, requires annual treatment, not hurricane-rated.

Prefabricated membrane (APP/SBS): $350–$600/m². Liquid-applied polyurethane: $250–$500/m². Double-layer system (membrane + liquid): $500–$900/m². A 150m² roof: $52,000–$135,000 MXN. We recommend double-layer for maximum protection. 10-year warranty.

Single-layer membrane: every 5–7 years. Double-layer system: 8–12 years. Liquid-applied alone: 3–5 years. UV exposure and thermal cycling are the main degradation factors. Annual inspection extends membrane life. Ponding water (from clogged drains) is the #1 cause of premature failure.

1) Insufficient slope — water pools on flat areas. 2) Clogged drains — tropical debris blocks drainage. 3) Membrane puncture — from foot traffic, furniture, antenna mounts. 4) Failed flashings — where roof meets walls or penetrations. 5) Age — UV degrades membrane. All preventable with proper design and maintenance.

Our 6-Step Process

01
Roof Inspection

Assess existing roof condition, drainage patterns, membrane status. Identify leak sources.

02
Design

Slope correction, drain placement, membrane selection, flashing details. Every penetration detailed.

03
Surface Preparation

Clean, repair cracks, prime surface. Remove old failed membrane if applicable.

04
Membrane Application

Primary membrane layer applied. Flashings at all penetrations and edges. Seam testing.

05
Second Layer

Liquid-applied topcoat for double protection. UV-reflective coating to reduce thermal stress.

06
Drainage Verification

Test all drains, verify slope with water test, install drain guards. No ponding at any point.
