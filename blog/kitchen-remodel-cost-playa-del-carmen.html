Timeline

Budget remodel: 2–3 weeks. Mid-range: 3–5 weeks. Premium/custom: 5–8 weeks.

What Adds the Most Value?

  1. Granite/quartz countertops — Instantly modernizes the space. ROI: 80–100%.
  2. Custom cabinetry — Local carpenters in Playa produce quality work at 40–60% less than US prices.
  3. Open layout — Removing a wall ($500–$1,500 if non-structural) dramatically improves flow.
  4. LED under-cabinet lighting — $200–$500. Makes every kitchen look premium in photos.

Kitchen Materials: Local vs Imported

Best value local: Mexican granite, handmade tile (talavera), local hardwood cabinets.

Our 6-Step Process

Frequently Asked Questions

Basic refresh: $80,000–$150,000 MXN. Mid-range (cabinets, countertops, appliances): $150,000–$300,000. Premium: $300,000–$600,000+.

Granite: $2,500–$5,000/m², heat and moisture resistant, best value. Quartz: $3,500–$7,000/m², non-porous. Avoid laminate (swells) and wood (mold).

Cabinet replacement: 1–2 weeks. Full remodel: 3–5 weeks. Custom build with structural changes: 4–8 weeks.

Local hardwood (tzalam, parota): $15,000–$40,000/linear meter, naturally termite-resistant. Imported MDF swells in 80% humidity. Local is better quality AND value.

Commercial-grade range hood (600+ CFM) vented to exterior is essential. Without it, cooking moisture accelerates mold. Budget: $8,000–$25,000 MXN.

Basic refresh: $80,000–$150,000 MXN. Mid-range (new cabinets, countertops, appliances): $150,000–$300,000. Premium (custom everything, imported): $300,000–$600,000+.

Granite: $2,500–$5,000/m², heat and moisture resistant, best value. Quartz: $3,500–$7,000/m², non-porous, zero maintenance. Avoid: laminate (swells with humidity), wood (mold risk).

Essential. Install commercial-grade range hood (600+ CFM) vented to exterior. Without proper ventilation, cooking moisture accelerates mold growth. Budget: $8,000–$25,000 MXN.
