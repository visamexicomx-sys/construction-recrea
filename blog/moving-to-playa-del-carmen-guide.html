Moving to Playa del Carmen: the Complete 2026 Relocation Guide

Digital nomads, families and retirees are moving here at record rates. Here is everything the brochures skip: real budgets, real neighborhoods, real paperwork.

The short version: a single person lives well on $850–$1,400 USD/month, a family of four on $1,950–$3,300. Renting a 2-bed: $450–$1,100/month — which is why long-stayers eventually build.

Monthly budgets (real 2026 numbers)

ProfileMXN/monthUSD/month
Single person$15,000–$25,000$850–$1,400
Couple$25,000–$40,000$1,400–$2,200
Family of 4$35,000–$60,000$1,950–$3,300

Rent (2-bed): $8,000–$20,000 MXN. Groceries: $4,000–$8,000. Fiber internet 100–500 Mbps: $400–$900 MXN. Healthcare: 50–70% below US prices.

Visas in one minute

Visas & residency in depth: Our partner guide MexVisa Pro covers every visa path: Temporary residency guide · Tourist FMM & 180 days
  • Tourist (FMM): up to 180 days — enough to scout, buy land, even start building
  • Temporary resident: income ~$4,300+ USD/month or savings ~$72,000+; renewable to 4 years, then permanent
  • Permanent: directly with ~$290,000+ USD savings (2026 thresholds — verify current numbers with a facilitator)

Schools, healthcare, day-to-day

  • Schools: international/bilingual options $3,000–$12,000 MXN/month (Colegio Puerto Aventuras, El Papalote, Génesis...)
  • Healthcare: Hospiten and Costamed in town; major surgery hubs in Cancún 45 min away
  • Getting around: most of Centro is walkable; a car matters for families in northern colonias
  • Safety: tourist and residential zones are well-policed; standard city awareness applies

Rent first, then build (the 2-year path)

Smart relocators rent for 6–12 months while scouting land. After year two, rent money is dead money: $15,000 MXN/month = $180,000 MXN/year — 7% of a small house's construction budget, gone. Building a 120 m² home costs $120,000–$170,000 USD and locks in your cost of living forever. Run your numbers or read Build vs Buy.
