Construction Permits in Tulum (2026)

Tulum is the most demanding permit jurisdiction in the Riviera Maya. Every project needs a municipal license and — crucially — a SEMA environmental authorization. Here is exactly what you need, what it costs and how long it takes in 2026.

Permits You Need in Tulum

  • Uso de suelo (land-use certificate) confirming your project is allowed on the lot
  • Licencia de construcción — municipal building license from the Tulum City Hall
  • SEMA environmental authorization — mandatory for every build in Tulum municipality
  • Land-clearing (desmonte) permit for jungle lots, with compensatory reforestation
  • DRO — a licensed Responsible Works Director must sign the project
  • CAPA feasibility for water/drainage (many lots need a cistern and treatment)

The Process Step by Step

  • Project and structural drawings signed by a licensed architect and DRO
  • Apply for uso de suelo at the municipality
  • File the SEMA environmental study and await authorization
  • Submit the building license application with all documents
  • Pay municipal fees and receive the license
  • On-site inspections during construction, then occupancy

Why SEMA Matters Most in Tulum

Unlike Playa del Carmen or Cancún, every project in the municipality of Tulum requires SEMA environmental approval — even a modest home. Skipping it risks fines, stop-work orders and problems registering the property. Jungle and cenote-adjacent lots need extra studies and reforestation. Budget 2–4 months before breaking ground.
