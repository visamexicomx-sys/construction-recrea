Home Renovation in Playa del Carmen: What It Costs and How to Plan

Whether you've just bought a property that needs updating or you've lived in your home for years and want a fresh look, renovation in Playa del Carmen has its own set of rules. Materials weather differently in the tropics, certain finishes hold up better than others, and permit requirements apply even for interior work.

Renovation Costs in Playa del Carmen (2026)

Kitchen Remodel

  • Basic refresh (paint, hardware, backsplash): $50,000–$100,000 MXN
  • Full remodel (new cabinets, countertops, appliances, tile): $150,000–$350,000 MXN
  • Luxury kitchen (custom carpentry, quartz/granite, imported appliances): $400,000–$700,000+ MXN

Bathroom Remodel

  • Basic (new fixtures, tile, paint): $40,000–$80,000 MXN
  • Full remodel (layout change, new plumbing, custom vanity): $100,000–$250,000 MXN

Full Home Renovation

  • Cosmetic update (paint, floors, fixtures): $3,000–$5,000 MXN/m²
  • Moderate renovation (structural changes, new installations): $6,000–$12,000 MXN/m²
  • Gut renovation (strip to structure, complete rebuild): $10,000–$18,000 MXN/m²

Most Popular Renovation Projects

Adding a Pool

Many homes in Playa del Carmen don't come with pools, and adding one is the single biggest value-add for both personal enjoyment and rental income. A standard 4x8m pool runs $250,000–$500,000 MXN installed.

Roof Terrace / Rooftop

Converting a flat roof into a usable rooftop terrace with pergola, wet bar, and jacuzzi is extremely popular. Budget $200,000–$500,000 MXN depending on features.

Outdoor Kitchen / BBQ Area

The tropical climate makes outdoor living spaces essential. A built-in BBQ area with counter, sink, and seating: $80,000–$200,000 MXN.

Hurricane-Proofing

Upgrading windows to hurricane-rated glass, reinforcing doors, and adding storm shutters. Essential for properties in Quintana Roo. Budget $100,000–$300,000 MXN for a full home.

Materials That Work in the Riviera Maya

  • Chukum plaster — traditional Mayan waterproof plaster, ideal for pools and exterior walls
  • Polished concrete floors — durable, cool underfoot, minimal maintenance
  • Tropical hardwoods — tzalam, machiche, and parota resist humidity and insects
  • Aluminum windows — won't rust like iron in salt air; go for hurricane-rated
  • Porcelain tile — better than ceramic for high-humidity environments

Do You Need a Permit for Renovation?

In Playa del Carmen, you need a construction permit for any work that modifies the structure — adding rooms, changing walls, modifying the roof, or extending the footprint. Cosmetic work (paint, flooring, fixtures) typically doesn't require permits, but electrical and plumbing modifications may need inspection.

Timeline

  • Kitchen remodel: 3–6 weeks
  • Bathroom remodel: 2–4 weeks
  • Full home renovation: 2–6 months
  • Pool addition: 4–8 weeks
