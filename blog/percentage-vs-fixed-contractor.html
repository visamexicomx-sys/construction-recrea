Percentage vs Fixed-Price Construction Contracts in Mexico

Updated June 2026 • By Recrea Construction • 8 min read

Bottom line: For most residential projects in Playa del Carmen, fixed-price contracts protect you better. Percentage contracts can work but require constant supervision and accounting oversight.

How Each Contract Type Works

Percentage (Cost-Plus)

You pay the actual cost of materials and labor, plus a percentage (typically 10-20%) as the contractor's fee. The contractor manages the project and charges their margin on top of all expenses.

Your total cost = (Materials + Labor) × (1 + Contractor %)

Example: $1,000,000 MXN in costs × 1.15 (15% fee) = $1,150,000 MXN total

Fixed-Price (Lump Sum)

You agree on a total price upfront for a defined scope of work. The contractor is responsible for managing costs within that budget. If costs exceed the estimate, the contractor absorbs the difference. If costs come in under, the contractor keeps the savings.

Your total cost = Agreed price (period)

The Hidden Problem with Percentage Contracts

The fundamental issue with percentage contracts is the misaligned incentive: the more your project costs, the more the contractor earns. A contractor on 15% earns $150,000 MXN on a $1 million project, but $225,000 MXN on a $1.5 million project.

This creates subtle (or not-so-subtle) behaviors:

  • No motivation to negotiate material prices — paying retail for cement instead of wholesale costs you more and earns the contractor a higher fee.
  • "Upgrades" that benefit the contractor — suggesting more expensive options that increase the project cost and therefore the contractor's percentage.
  • Scope creep tolerance — no pushback when the project expands, because expansion increases the fee.
  • Inflated labor costs — keeping more workers on site than necessary, or allowing work to stretch beyond reasonable timelines.

When Percentage Contracts Make Sense

  • Renovation with unknown scope — when you don't know what's behind the walls until you open them. Older properties in Playa del Carmen often reveal surprises.
  • You have extensive construction experience — if you can spot inflated costs, verify material prices, and manage the construction process yourself.
  • You trust the contractor implicitly — based on multiple previous projects together, not just a recommendation.
  • The project is small — on a $50,000 MXN repair job, the margin difference is small enough that the flexibility benefit outweighs the cost risk.

How to Protect Yourself with Either Contract

  1. Detailed scope of work — whether fixed or percentage, every material specification, dimension, and finish should be documented.
  2. Milestone payments — never pay more than 30% upfront. Tie remaining payments to verified completed phases.
  3. Change order process — any scope change must be agreed in writing with a price impact before work begins.
  4. Independent verification — on percentage contracts, hire an independent quantity surveyor to audit costs quarterly.
  5. Cap on percentage contracts — if using cost-plus, set a maximum cap (guaranteed maximum price, or GMP) above which the contractor absorbs all additional costs.
