Frequently Asked Questions

CAPA (municipal water) service is unreliable — outages last 4–48 hours, especially in high season. Water pressure drops during peak hours. In Tulum, many areas have NO CAPA service at all. A cistern guarantees 3–7 days of water independence.

Rule of thumb: 150L per person per day × days of reserve. 2-person home: 5,000–10,000L. Family of 4: 10,000–20,000L. Airbnb property: 15,000–25,000L. Boutique hotel: 30,000–100,000L. We calculate based on bedrooms, bathrooms, pool, and garden.

Concrete underground (most common): $25,000–$80,000 MXN depending on size. Rotoplas (plastic tank, above-ground): $5,000–$25,000. Rainwater harvesting system: $40,000–$120,000 MXN including filtration. Underground is preferred — keeps water cool, saves space.

Excellent region for it — 1,200–1,500mm annual rainfall. A 100m² roof captures 120,000–150,000L/year. First-flush diverter + sediment filter + UV sterilization = potable-quality water. SEMA in Tulum gives credits for rainwater harvesting in your environmental permit.

At minimum: sediment filter + carbon filter for CAPA water. For well water: add reverse osmosis ($15,000–$35,000 MXN). For drinking: UV sterilization ($8,000–$15,000). Full whole-house system (sediment + carbon + softener + UV): $30,000–$60,000 MXN.

Our 6-Step Process

01
Water Assessment

Analyze your property's CAPA availability, water quality (TDS test), consumption needs, and rainwater potential.

02
System Design

Cistern sizing, placement, pump selection, treatment chain. Integrate with house plumbing design.

03
Excavation

Underground cisterns require careful excavation in limestone. Jackhammer + excavator. 2–4 days.

04
Construction

Reinforced concrete cistern with waterproof coating. Or Rotoplas installation for above-ground.

05
Plumbing Integration

Float valve from CAPA, pump to house, overflow drain, access hatch. Backflow prevention.

06
Treatment & Commissioning

Install filters, UV, RO as needed. Test water quality. Train owner on filter replacement schedule.
