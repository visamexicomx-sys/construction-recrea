Rooftop Terrace: The Best Investment in Playa del Carmen

June 2026 • Recrea Construction • 5 min read

Fact: a rooftop terrace with plunge pool increases Airbnb nightly rate by $40–$80. Payback: under 1 year with consistent bookings.

Why a Rooftop Terrace Is a Must-Have

Most homes in Playa del Carmen and Tulum have flat concrete roofs — unused space that can become the main living area. A rooftop gives you:

  • Extra living space without changing the building footprint
  • Views of jungle, city, or ocean — the most photogenic feature for Airbnb
  • Privacy — unlike the yard, the rooftop is hidden from neighbors
  • Breeze — noticeably cooler at height in the heat

Structural Requirements

Load Capacity

Standard concrete roofs (losa) in Mexico are designed for 200–350 kg/m². Sufficient for a terrace with furniture and people. For a rooftop pool, reinforcement is needed — adding beams or columns. Engineering calculation is mandatory.

Waterproofing

Critical. A roof leak means flooding the living spaces below. We use modified asphalt membranes (APP/SBS), polyurethane liquid waterproofing, and drainage layers under the finish.

Railings & Safety

Minimum railing height: 90 cm per Mexican code. For rental properties we recommend 110 cm. Materials: steel railings + glass or plastered parapet walls.

Best Features for Airbnb

  1. Plunge pool with lighting — the #1 booking magnet
  2. Hammocks and daybeds — Tulum style, essential for photos
  3. Tropical plants in pots — palms, monstera, bougainvillea
  4. LED string lights and spotlights — transform the terrace at night
  5. BBQ zone / wet bar — extends time guests spend on the rooftop

Build Timeline

  • Basic terrace: 2–3 weeks
  • With pergola: 3–4 weeks
  • With plunge pool: 5–7 weeks
  • Full rooftop: 6–10 weeks
