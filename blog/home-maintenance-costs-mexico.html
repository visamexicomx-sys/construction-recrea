How to Reduce Costs

  1. Solar panels — cut electricity bill by 60–80%. Payback in 4–5 years.
  2. Inverter A/C units — use half the energy of standard units.
  3. LED lighting — saves $28–$55 USD/month.
  4. Rainwater collection — reduces water usage by 30–50%.
  5. Roof insulation — reduces heat and A/C demand.

1–2% of property value per year. $3M MXN home: $30,000–$60,000/year. Main items: waterproofing check, A/C service, pest control, paint touchup, plumbing inspection, electrical check, pool/garden maintenance. Tropical climate accelerates wear — maintenance is not optional.

1) Roof waterproofing — check annually, repair immediately. 2) A/C service — every 6 months (salt air corrodes coils). 3) Pest control — monthly (tropical termites, scorpions). 4) Plumbing — annual inspection for scale buildup. 5) Exterior paint — every 3–5 years (UV and salt degradation).

Every 3–5 years for coastal properties, 5–7 years inland. Use elastomeric paint (flexes with temperature changes, resists cracking). Cost: $40–$80/m² for quality paint and labor. A 150m² home exterior: $25,000–$50,000 MXN per repaint. Lighter colors last longer (less UV absorption).

Tropical termites are aggressive — they can damage untreated wood structures in 2–3 years. Prevention: annual professional fumigation ($1,500–$3,000 MXN), use tropical hardwood (naturally resistant), treat all wood with borate solution, keep wood away from soil contact. Prevention costs 1% of replacement.

If you live full-time: DIY basics + contractors for specialized work. If absent or renting: hire professional property maintenance ($3,000–$8,000 MXN/month). They handle: weekly inspections, emergency repairs, cleaning, pest control coordination, vendor management. Essential for rental properties.
