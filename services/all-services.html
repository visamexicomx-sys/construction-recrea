Permits & Licenses

We handle the entire permit process. Our team has direct relationships with municipal offices across the Riviera Maya.

Land Use Permit

Municipal zoning verification and land use authorization. Required before any construction begins.

Timeline: 2–4 weeks · Cost: $500–$1,500 USD

Construction License

Official construction permit from the municipality. Includes DRO (Director Responsable de Obra) sign-off.

Timeline: 4–8 weeks · Cost: $1,500–$4,000 USD

Environmental Impact

MIA (Manifestación de Impacto Ambiental) for lots in ecological zones or near mangroves.

Timeline: 8–16 weeks · Cost: $3,000–$8,000 USD

CONAGUA Water Permit

Required for wells, cenote proximity, or water discharge. We handle the entire federal process.

Timeline: 6–12 weeks · Cost: $2,000–$5,000 USD

CFE Electrical Connection

Grid connection application, meter installation, and temporary construction power.

Timeline: 2–6 weeks · Cost: $300–$1,000 USD

Habitability Certificate

Final municipal inspection certifying the building meets code. Required for occupancy and utilities.

Timeline: 2–4 weeks · Cost: $500–$1,500 USD

Fideicomiso for Foreigners

Foreigners cannot directly own property within 50 km of the coast in Mexico. The fideicomiso (bank trust) gives you full ownership rights — buy, sell, renovate, rent, inherit. We coordinate the entire process:

  • Bank selection & application
  • SRE (Foreign Affairs) permit
  • Notary coordination
  • Title search & due diligence
  • RFC (tax ID) registration
  • Annual trust renewal management

Cost: $1,500–$3,000 USD setup · $500–$800 USD/year · Timeline: 6–12 weeks
